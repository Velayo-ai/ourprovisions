Subject: OurProvisions is in real hands — here's what's ready

Hi friends,

Quick update from the boat: OurProvisions is officially on real phones this month, with real people sharing real grocery lists. That's a big line to cross — going from "it works" to "someone is using it to shop" — and I wanted to walk you through the parts that are ready now and why each one earns its place.

**The shared list.** You and whoever you shop with are on the same list, and changes move between you almost instantly. This is the whole foundation — a grocery list only gets interesting the moment two people are on it, and making that feel trustworthy is most of the work. Everything below is really in service of that.

**Add-and-go quantities.** Tap to add an item, and it becomes a simple up/down counter. Take it to zero and it tidies itself back to a single "Add" button — no stray "0"s cluttering your list. Little thing, but the whole app is made of little things done right.

**One-tap declutter.** Lists get noisy mid-shop. One button cycles your view: everything by aisle → hide what you've already grabbed → a flat A–Z when you just need to find one thing. It stays invisible until you want it.

**Invite someone aboard.** You can hand your list straight to the person you shop with, right from inside the app. This is the one I care about most — the app was always meant to be shared, and this is the moment it becomes shared. (One of our testers did this on his very first try, completely unprompted. Made my week.)

**Staples.** Mark the things your household always needs so your list starts to learn your baseline.

I'll be honest about the rough edges too, because you're the people I trust to hear it: we found a few sync hiccups when two people are on one list at the same time, and a couple of moments where the app didn't explain itself well enough (it installs right from the web — no app store needed — and I hadn't made that clear enough). Those are top of the fix list right now. Finding them is exactly why you're here.

If you'd like to be one of the first households on it, just reply and I'll get you aboard. And if you're already using it — tell me what's clumsy. That feedback is the fuel.

More soon.
