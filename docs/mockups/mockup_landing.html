The problem

Nobody has ever enjoyed a grocery run.

Shopping without a list takes longer, costs more, and you still get home missing the one thing you went for. Shopping with a list means somebody had to build the list — and that somebody is always the same somebody.

So most households run on a half-remembered list in one person's head, and everyone else guesses. You get home with the oat milk. So does your partner. Or nobody does.

Here's the part nobody says out loud: you don't need a budget to control what you spend on food. You need a list. The impulse buy is what happens when you're wandering. Groceries are one of the biggest checks your household writes every month, and it's the one nobody's actually running.

We called it provisions, not groceries, for a reason. When you stock a boat you can't run back to the store — so you plan well, waste less, and get it right together. That's what we're bringing home to the kitchen.

So we built the list your whole household can run together — and it remembers what you buy.
Aboard today

Built small, on purpose.

Real households have been shopping on this since June. Everything here earned its place by being watched.

Shop it down to nothing

Check things off as you go and watch the list shrink. Whoever else is shopping sees it happen — so nobody's buying the milk twice, and you both know exactly what's left.

It already knows your regulars

Everything your household actually buys, in one place. Add what you need in a tap. Mark the things you always run out of and they stop being your problem to remember.

Hand it to your crew

Add the people you live with and the list is theirs too — same list, same moment, whoever's at the store. Run more than one house? Each keeps its own. A list one person keeps is a chore. A list everyone keeps is just how the house runs.
